# Data Storytelling Reflection

## Question:
If you notice a sharp spike in revenue in one specific region but only for a specific category, what secondary data (e.g., weather, local holidays, competitor ads) would you investigate to explain this anomaly?

## Answer:
When observing a localized, category-specific spike in revenue, the goal is to determine if the cause is internal (our actions) or external (market conditions). Here is the secondary data I would investigate:

1. **Marketing & Promotional Data**: Check if there were any targeted ad campaigns, email blasts, or promotions specifically launched in that region for that category. Sometimes marketing teams run localized A/B tests that cause sudden spikes.
2. **Local Holidays & Events**: Identify if there is a regional holiday, festival, or major local event. For example, a spike in "Apparel" in one region could coincide with a local sporting event or cultural festival.
3. **Weather Patterns**: Extreme or sudden weather changes can drive category-specific purchases. A sudden cold snap might spike "Home" or "Apparel" (winter wear) sales in a specific area.
4. **Competitor Behavior**: Investigate if a key regional competitor experienced an outage, raised prices, or received bad PR, which may have driven their customers to us.
5. **Macro-Economic/Regulatory Changes**: Check for any sudden changes in local regulations or economic stimuli (e.g., tech subsidies) that might make the category more appealing or affordable in that specific region.
6. **Platform/Technical Issues**: Ensure it's not a bug. Sometimes a pricing glitch or a currency conversion error can mimic a revenue spike.
